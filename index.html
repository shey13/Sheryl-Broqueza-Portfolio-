CASE STUDY 01

SKU-Led vs Editorial Banner Creative

CROA/B TestOnsite

+206%
CTR Uplift (Men)
+148%
NMV Uplift (Women)

The Problem

ZALORA’s onsite banners relied on editorial/lifestyle imagery — aspirational but abstract. Users scanned past banners without engaging because the creative didn’t show them what they could buy.

The Hypothesis

Showing actual product SKUs (real items at real prices) would outperform editorial imagery by reducing cognitive load. Users scan banners in ~2.6 seconds — SKU imagery provides faster comprehension.

Iteration Timeline

MAY 2022 · WOMEN
Initial 1-day test. SKU-led vs Editorial. Massive uplift confirmed.
+435% CTR · +148% NMV · +34% CR
MAY 2022 · MEN
Same-day test on Men segment. Strong uplift across all metrics.
+206% CTR · +26% NMV · +31% CR
FEB 2023 · EXTENDED 4-DAY
Extended rerun. CTR held (+47% Women, +23% Men) but CR dropped due to high click volume. Key learning: high CTR does not equal high CR at scale.
CTR held up · CR dropped at volume

The Learning

SKU-led creative drives dramatically higher engagement — but at volume, CR can decline as more browsers enter the funnel. Solution: SKU banners for flash sales (high intent), editorial for always-on (brand building). Codified in ZALORA’s creative playbook.

Why This Matters

The extended rerun’s “failure” was equally valuable — it prevented a blanket rollout that would have degraded CR. The output was a nuanced, context-dependent creative strategy.

CASE STUDY 02

Daily Style Feed — Personalized Discovery at Scale

The Problem

Standard homepage RecFeed: 26.98% CTR, 28.29% add-to-cart — plateauing. Users with zero or low product detail views were bouncing without discovering products.

The Solution

Built a dedicated “Daily Style Feed” on the app home screen — curated, campaign-aligned, full content flexibility. Routed low/zero-PDV users via dedicated onsite pages. Pure product-first discovery.

Architecture

  • Discovery engine on app home screen with personalized product exposure
  • Full content flexibility — no category or homepage logic constraints
  • Low-PDV user routing via dedicated onsite pages
  • Social media channels augmenting awareness and traffic
  • Rolled out live across all ZALORA markets

The Results

Nearly doubled CTR vs standard RecFeed (52% vs 26.98%). Full funnel: 48.5% product click → 98.5% to product view → 30.9% PDP-to-cart → 5.5% attributed purchase. Now permanent across all markets.

Why This Matters

Not a one-off test — a permanent product live across every ZALORA market. A structural improvement to product discovery infrastructure.

CASE STUDY 03

The Problem

Voucher banners displayed promo codes directly. Was showing the code upfront helping conversion, or adding visual noise and reducing click incentive?

The Hypothesis

Removing the visible code and adding a clear CTA (“Shop Now”) would increase CTR and CR by creating stronger forward momentum. Code auto-applies at checkout.

Iteration Timeline

APR 17, 2023 · FLASH SALE
First test: CTA banner vs Code banner. 1–7 PM flash. Women/Men/Kids.
+14.53% CTR · +9% CR · +14% NMV
APR 20, 2023 · FLASH V2
Repeat with different time window (2–8 PM). Confirmed direction.
+6.98% CTR · +6% CR · +48% NMV
MAY 2–4, 2023 · BASELINE
Extended 1-week baseline. Validated effect holds beyond flash urgency.
+4.69% CTR · +2% CR · +48% NMV

The Learning

“Without Code + CTA” won across all three iterations. Not a novelty effect. Now standard for voucher campaigns.

Why This Matters

Demonstrates iterated validation — three progressively different conditions. A small UX change unlocked double-digit revenue uplift at zero cost.

CASE STUDY 04

The Problem

App Grid content across 6 segments updated manually every week — 30 min/segment, 3 hours/cycle. Error-prone, timing risks for critical campaigns. Scaling to 10+ segments was unsustainable.

The Solution

Built an n8n workflow: ingest approved copy → validate segment mapping → transform to API schema → batch update with retry, logging, Slack alerts, and rollback. Also built a trend intelligence pipeline (n8n + GPT + TikTok + Pinterest).

Business Case

  • Build cost: €920 (10 mandays)
  • Labour savings: €936/year (156 hours eliminated)
  • Campaign uplift protected: €10,000/year (5 campaigns × €200K × 1%)
  • Error reduction: €216/year
  • Scalability: €624/year (4 new segments, no headcount)
  • Net first-year return: €10,928 — 1,187.8% ROI

The Learning

The biggest win was protecting campaign timing. Reframing from “saves 3 hours/week” to “protects €10K/year” got CEO approval. Always tie automation to revenue impact.

Why This Matters

Shows ability to think commercially about technical solutions — building both the ROI model and the actual implementation.

The Problem

Reactivation campaigns for app-installed users delivered 0.70% CTR — generic messaging didn’t account for user behavior. High install base, low re-engagement.

The Solution

Revamped with personalized messaging and product recs tailored to browse/purchase history. Doubled CTR to 1.49% (PH/ID), up to 4% in SG, MY, HK. Downstream: 50% click-to-browse, 29% browse-to-ATC — genuine intent, not empty clicks.

Methodology

03
